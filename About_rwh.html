About Food Resources



Food resources means all commodities and products (simple, mixed, or compound), or complements to such commodities or products, that are capable of being ingested by either human beings or animals, irrespective of other uses to which such commodities or products may be put, at all stages of processing from the raw commodity to the products thereof in vendible form for human or animal consumption. ‘‘Food resources’’ also means potable water packaged in commercially marketable containers, all starches, sugars, vegetable and animal or marine fats and oils, seed, cotton, hemp, and flax fiber, but does not mean any such material after it loses its identity as an agricultural commodity or agricultural product.

What is Food Resource?

So, what is food resource? Food resource refers to any substance or material that can be used to sustain or nourish living organisms, including humans, animals, and plants. Food resources can be natural or synthetic, and they can come in many different forms, such as fruits, vegetables, grains, meat, dairy, and processed foods. The availability and accessibility of food resources can have a significant impact on the health and well-being of individuals and communities. In some cases, food resources may be scarce or inaccessible due to factors such as poverty, natural disasters, or political instability, leading to malnutrition and other health problems. Effective management of food resources is therefore essential to ensure food security and promote healthy lifestyles..
